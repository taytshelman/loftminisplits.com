Rebates in 2026: what is real and what expired

The federal tax credit is gone. The 25C credit that paid up to $2,000 on heat pumps expired December 31, 2025. Any site still advertising it for 2026 installs is running stale content. Do not count on it.

What Wichita homeowners can actually get right now: Evergy pays a $150 rebate on a heat pump mini split that replaces an existing mini split or is added to a home with electric heating, per Evergy's official 2026 Kansas rebate chart. Equipment must be AHRI-certified, and the program runs through 2027. Kansas also has a federally funded home rebate program in the pipeline that has not launched yet; when it goes live for income-qualified households, we will update this page.

What it costs to run

This is where mini splits quietly win. A single zone typically runs $30 to $60 a month in electricity, versus $100 to $200 a month for whole-home central air. Modern units carry SEER2 ratings of 18 to 30 against the 14 to 16 of standard central systems, and because there are no ducts, none of your heating or cooling leaks into the attic. ENERGY STAR puts duct losses at more than 30 percent when ducts run through unconditioned space. Heating a room you are in, instead of pushing air through ducts to the whole house, is simply cheaper physics.

Wichita mini split cost questions

How much does a mini split cost installed in Wichita?
At Loft, a standard single-room 12,000 BTU install starts at $985. National guides put typical single-zone installs at $2,000 to $5,000, so get a specialist quote before assuming the national number.
How much does a multi-zone system cost?
Midwest installers typically charge $6,000 to $9,000 for two zones and $9,000 to $12,000 for three. Multi-zone pricing depends on sizing and line runs, so we quote those individually, for free, usually in one call.
Is there a Wichita rebate in 2026?
Yes. Evergy's official 2026 chart pays $150 for a qualifying heat pump mini split. The federal 25C credit expired at the end of 2025 and no longer applies.
What size mini split do I need?
Roughly 25 BTU per square foot: 9k BTU to about 350 sq ft, 12k to about 550, 18k to about 1,000. Kansas winters push sizing up 10 to 20 percent if the unit is your primary heat.
Is DIY cheaper?
On paper. DIY kits run $1,700 to $4,000 all-in, but most equipment warranties are void without a licensed installer, refrigerant work legally requires EPA 608 certification on most brands, and many HVAC companies will not finish a stalled DIY job. A $985 professional single-zone install closes most of that gap for none of the risk.
